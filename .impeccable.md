## Design Context

### Users
This app is for home and office users who want to add a TV once, label it by scene such as living room, bedroom, or company, and then quickly control it without setup friction. Users may be standing near a TV and expect large, reliable controls that work with one hand.

### Brand Personality
The interface should feel calm, precise, and dependable. It should look like a polished commercial Android utility rather than a marketing page or experimental concept.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a Material Design 3 light theme with neutral surfaces, restrained teal accents, red-orange only for power/destructive emphasis, clear hierarchy, and no decorative gradients or distorted mockup effects. Screens should be directly implementable with Android XML/ViewBinding and Material Components.

### Design Principles
- Prioritize fast control: the first useful action should be visible without reading instructions.
- Use scene labels to make devices memorable and human, but keep metadata compact.
- Keep tappable controls large enough for repeated use and one-handed operation.
- Avoid visual tricks that cannot ship cleanly in native Android views.
- Separate browsing/setup screens from the remote screen so each page has one obvious job.
